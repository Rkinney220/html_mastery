The United States of America won its freedom from Britain in 1783 in the treaty of Paris signed by represenatives from The USA, UK, France, The Netherlands, and Spain (All US allies technically singed seperate treaties with Britain but historians often refer to all treaties as just the peace of paris). The initial governing document of the United States was the Articles of Confederation, an incredibly weak and ineffective form of government that a singular state could shutdown the entire process.


However due to demonstrations of government ineffectivenes & weaknes with incidents like the Whiskey Rebelion and Shays Rebelion founding fathers such as Alexander Hamiltion, Thomas Jefferson, and many more all agreed that the government needed to be stronger, and thus the first constitutional convention (and only legal one) was called to hammer a new compromise government between the Democratic-Republicans led by Jefferson who prefered a smaller weaker government than the Federalists led by Hamilton who favored a british like strong central government. Many other compromises had to be adopted between north and south. Mostly famously, the selection of D.C. as the capital of the nation, and the three fiths compromise regarding slaves. James Madison, eventually became one of the documents most influential architechts, being named the father of the consitution for his efforts.


On the 17th of September 1787, the document was finalized, and presented to the 13 states for ratification, which ended with its ratification by New Hampshire became the 9th state to ratify it thus setting it over its selected threshold by the government for it's adoption.
